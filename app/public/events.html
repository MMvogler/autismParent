Sensory Friendly Events


All Year Round

JumpStreet has locations in Murfreesboro, Franklin and Goodlettsville. They offer a private hour of jumping for children with special needs and this is for children of all ages.


NCG Cinema in Gallatin does sensory-friendly movie screenings of the latest Pixar/Disney movies in a dim, softer sound, no previews environment. The best part is kids are allowed to run around and make noise, which I think is amazing!


The Adventure Science Museum offers sensory-friendly planetarium shows once a month. They dim the lights, lower the sound, reduce the crowds and allow for running around and noise. The most impressive part are the amenities they offer: weighted blankets, stuffed animals, noise reduction ear muffs and even sensory kits you can borrow! I'm so impressed by their thoroughness!


Spring

At Andrew Jackson's Hermitage they have a Presidential Easter Egg Hunt every year. They schedule in a sensory friendly one at the end of the day for kids with special needs. It's usually about $8 per person and they have additional crafty activities on site as well.


Longview Recreation Center in Spring Hill has an Egg Dive , where special needs children can dive into the pool to find eggs. Kids can meet the Easter Bunny afterwards as well.


The Rivergate Mall designates some time specifically for families who have special needs children, so that they too can get a Easter Bunny picture. They open up their doors before the mall opens for this event and they usually offer it with Santa at Christmas as well.
