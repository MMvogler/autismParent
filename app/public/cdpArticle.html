Charlie Daniels Park


This is really the best park for kids in Mount Juliet! The slides are huge and there is always a swing available for my daughter. There are two things that you should know in advance about this park, particularly if you have a child with ASD. First, they have a wonderful splash pad that is open in the Summer. However, they have a surprisingly strict rule that no children can run in the splash pad area.

Every kid in there gets so excited that they inevitably get caught up in the excitement and start to run. There is a teenager with a whistle that will tell your kid to stop. It’s a little sad seeing all the kids trying to temper their excitement, but I guess rules are rules.

Now I don’t know if your kid understands what’s happening when a person yells “STOP!” in their face, but my kid doesn’t understand or care and will continue to enjoy herself. I was the only parent who had to go into the water and hold their kid back. I didn’t realize I was going to have to do this. I was pretty soaked by the end of the experience, but luckily my phone and ego (from all the parents standing to the side staring at me) survived the incident and I never took her during the hours the splash pad was open (before 10am in the Summer last year).

Secondly, there is a section for the littlest kids that’s closed off, but if your child is a little older, they’ll go into the main play area. Know that the exits are wide open to the parking lot. It is easier to have more than one adult with you, so during the chaos and fun there’s someone to block most of the exits. You can definitely lose your kid if you’re not careful. I have heard this complaint not just from “Autism Parents” but also from parents with twins or just multiple high energy children. Come mentally prepared and/or in teams if you have 1 or more runners in your family.

It’s such a wonderful park, and who knows maybe they will find more lackadaisical employees to manage the splash pad this Summer. I can only hope!!
