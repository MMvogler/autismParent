Wave Country


We went here last year and our daughter had so much fun! Due to the fact that it’s run by Nashville’s Parks and Rec department, it is super affordable, which is a huge plus when you’re taking your kid somewhere for the first time. It could be a total bust, but I felt like we could give it a try for the price.

There’s a café on site that sells burgers and hotdogs, which is convenient. You can also bring a cooler of whatever you want. I’m assuming alcohol is against the rules, but I’m honestly not sure at this place. It has got a pretty relaxed atmosphere! There’s a really nice kitty pool, a big wave pool with plenty of free inner tubes, and a few large water slides for the bigger kids. It was clean and just what we needed for a day outside in water!

We did have to wait in a line to get in, which is always a struggle for my daughter. Even though we got there right when it opened. That’s always my trick to avoid lines anywhere I go, but that won’t work here. I believe that we could have rented a locker to keep our stuff in, and they did have picnic tables. There are some shady places, but everything is outside.

The biggest thing to prepare for if you have a child with sensory issues is the crowd. It can get pretty crowded at any point in the Summer, so if your kid has a hard time with a crowded and loud space, this might be a little stressful. Our daughter loves water so much that it was easy to push past that anxiety and adjust to the waves. She had a blast and we would definitely do it again!
