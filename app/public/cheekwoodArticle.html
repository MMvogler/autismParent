Cheekwood Estate and Gardens


I haven’t visited since they opened the Bracken Foundation Children’s Garden. I don’t have to see it to know that it is beautiful. Everything about this place is beautiful all year round, but especially in the Spring and Summer. A lot of parents who have experience visiting know to bring a wagon and pack a lunch for a picnic while they are there.

I don’t quiet have the most leisurely experience due to the fact that my daughter wants to jump in every pond, lake and fountain that she sees. When I tell her that she can’t she usually has a big meltdown, which involves screaming and kicking with inevitable confused looks from other families walking by. I know that I shouldn’t care, but I have to be honest that it makes me self-conscious every time.

She also enjoys grabbing flowers and ripping them apart, so this is the type of place that involves me playing a lot of defense. It is a lot of stress and effort on my part as the “autism parent.” I am at a full upper lip sweat after 15 minutes of being there. There is an indoor section that has a lot of “don’t touch” signs that my daughter is going to obviously ignore, so there is no real place to take her that doesn’t have some kind of tactile/destruction opportunity.

It’s so pretty though!! I still go maybe once or twice a year to maybe get a pretty Fall picture of her with the pumpkins or maybe I’m feeling down and I want to punish myself for no real reason. I just wear workout gear and pack water while I mentally prepare like I’m about to do a marathon. Then when I leave Cheekwood, I think about how I’m not coming back anytime soon.
