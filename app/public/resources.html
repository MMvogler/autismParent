STEP is a local Nashville nonprofit that helped me personally to figure out my kid's public school options when we first moved to Nashville. It stands for Support and Training for Exceptional Parents, and they offer "Special Education Assistance for Tennessee Families." I was knew to the Autism world while also being knew to the area, and they were able to explain things to me in a way that actually made sense in a very overwhelming time. Do you know your child's rights when it comes to their education? STEP can help. FIVE MF'in STARS!

I just started looking into GRANTS to help pay for our daughter's ABA therapy. The biggest stress my husband and I have had since our daughter’s diagnosis is money. People just don’t realize how expensive care is for a child with a disability. I will update this as I go.
- UnitedHealthcare Children’s Foundation = So far this is was the easiest process, and I even received a notice that I was approved for some money within a month. Click here for more information. Keep in mind: It takes 30 minutes to fill out once you have all of the appropriate documents and the money covers therapy that she’ll receive next year.
- C.A.R.E Autism Foundation = There is a quick 5-minute form you fill out. Then you have to wait for up to 8 weeks to see if they approve/deny your request. THEN you start the process. Click here for more information. Keep in mind: This foundation supports families nationally, so it isn’t just for people in California. I’m still in the 8-week waiting period for this one.
